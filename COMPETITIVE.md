| **KCCA / traditional haulers** | Council & private trucks | Coverage, mandate | Manual, no app, no transparency, no data |

> "Cowaste" does not appear as a public app — the real benchmark in Uganda is **Yo-Waste**,
> with **Homeklin** as the industrial incumbent and **T-Bin** as the smart-bin reference.

## Why EcoCycle wins — the 6 talking points

1. **It's an ecosystem, not a feature.** Yo-Waste moves trucks; Homeklin builds a plant; T-Bin sells bins. EcoCycle connects **citizens, collectors and the city** on one platform — the whole loop.
2. **Citizens become sensors.** Civic reporting + a live hotspot map turns every resident into a data source for cleaner streets — something no competitor offers. This directly attacks Kampala's drainage-blocking, flood-causing dumping.
3. **AI does the hard part.** Real on-device image recognition tells people *exactly* how to sort an item — removing the #1 barrier to recycling. Runs in the browser, even offline.
4. **Behaviour change through rewards.** Points, badges and a leaderboard make recycling habit-forming. Competitors have no engagement layer — EcoCycle drives the behaviour that actually increases recycling rates.
5. **Proof of impact for funders & the UN.** A built-in SDG dashboard (tonnes diverted, CO₂ saved, circular-economy value) gives donors and councils the transparency they need — turning waste into measurable climate action.
6. **Zero-friction reach.** As an installable PWA it needs no app-store, runs on any phone, and works offline — critical for Ugandan connectivity. Smart-bin features are software-first, so cities adopt without T-Bin's hardware capital cost.
