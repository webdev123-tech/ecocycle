1. Executive Summary

EcoCycle is a working software platform — not a mockup or a concept slide — that tackles one of Kampala's most dangerous and visible problems: solid waste. The city generates roughly 2,500 tonnes of waste every day, yet only about half is ever collected. The rest burns in open pits, blocks drainage channels and feeds disasters like the Kiteezi landfill collapse of 9 August 2024, which killed at least 35 people.

EcoCycle attacks the problem at its root cause — a broken information loop between the people who produce waste, the people who collect it, and the authorities who must manage it. It unifies all three into a single mobile-first application with real accounts, real data, on-device artificial intelligence for waste sorting, gamified recycling incentives, digital payments, and a live city-impact dashboard.

The platform directly advances three United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (11, 12 and 13) and out-performs existing Ugandan and regional solutions on coverage, intelligence and citizen engagement.

2. The Problem — Kampala's Waste Crisis

Kampala Capital City Authority (KCCA) estimates the city produces about 2,500 tonnes of solid waste per day, of which only around 1,200 tonnes are actually collected. Official collection coverage has improved from roughly 35% in 2016 to 59% in 2023, but that still leaves hundreds of tonnes per day in streets, wetlands and drainage channels.

For nearly three decades, almost all of that waste went to a single site: the Kiteezi landfill, opened in 1996 on about 36 acres, 12 km from the city centre. It reached its design capacity in 2012 but kept receiving 1,500–2,000 tonnes every day. On 9 August 2024, heavy rain triggered a collapse of the unstable garbage mountain that buried homes and people — at least 35 confirmed dead and 28 missing. The disaster forced the removal of KCCA's top leadership and exposed how fragile a one-landfill, low-visibility system really is.

Root cause: The crisis is not only physical, it is informational. Citizens have no easy channel to report illegal dumping or request a pickup; collectors work blind without digital job lists or proof of service; and city managers lack real-time data on where waste is accumulating. There is also almost no incentive to separate or recycle at the source, so recyclable plastic, organics and e-waste are dumped together and lost.

The gaps EcoCycle closes

  • No citizen reporting loop — illegal dumps go unreported and unaddressed.
  • No source separation — recyclables are mixed with general waste and buried.
  • Opaque collection — no tracking, no proof, no accountability for pickups.
  • No behaviour-change incentive — recycling is unrewarded, so it doesn't happen.
  • No real-time data — authorities cannot target hotspots or measure progress.

4. The Solution — EcoCycle

EcoCycle, tagged "Smart Waste, Clean Cities," is a single Progressive Web App (installable on phones and as an Android package) that serves three roles in one unified ecosystem:

👤 Citizen

Report waste with a photo and GPS location, get instant AI sorting guidance, schedule and pay for pickups, earn points and rewards for recycling, and learn through the education hub.

🚛 Collector

Receive a live job queue, navigate to reports, upload photographic proof of collection, and share live truck location — turning informal collection into an accountable service.

🏙️ City / Admin

Monitor reports and complaints on a live map, broadcast emergency alerts, and read an SDG impact dashboard that quantifies waste diverted and coverage achieved.

♻️ The loop

A citizen's report becomes a collector's job becomes a city data-point becomes a measurable SDG outcome — closing the information loop that the current system lacks.

6. Key Features

📸 Report & AI sort

Snap a photo of waste; on-device AI classifies it (plastic, organic, paper, glass, e-waste) and the report is geo-tagged for collectors.

🗓️ Schedules & pickups

Citizens schedule collections and pay online (Mobile Money / card via Flutterwave), or use the sandbox flow for demos.

🏆 Points, levels & rewards

Recycling and reporting earn points and levels; points redeem for rewards — a behaviour-change engine for SDG 12.

🚛 Collector jobs + proof

Live job queue, navigation, and photographic proof-of-collection bring accountability to every pickup.

🗺️ Live map & truck location

Leaflet/OpenStreetMap shows reports, hotspots and collection trucks in real time.

📊 SDG impact dashboard

Charts quantify waste diverted, recycling rates and collection coverage for the city / admin role.

🔔 Emergency alerts

Authorities broadcast alerts (overflowing sites, hazards) directly to citizens.

📚 Education hub

Guidance on sorting, composting and the circular economy — building climate capacity (SDG 13.3).

📴 Works offline

A service worker caches the app shell and the AI model, so the core experience survives poor connectivity.

♻️ Circular economy

A waste-to-resource view frames collected material as input for compost, recycling and energy — not just garbage.

7. The Intelligence Layer — On-Device Waste Sorting

EcoCycle's standout technical feature is artificial intelligence that runs entirely on the user's device. Using TensorFlow.js with a MobileNet vision model (vendored locally, ~14 MB), the app classifies a photographed item and maps it to a waste category — for example plastic bottle → plastic, banana peel → organic, old phone → e-waste.

Why on-device matters here

  • No cloud cost — classification is free, so it scales to millions of users without server bills.
  • Privacy-preserving — photos never have to leave the phone to be sorted.
  • Works offline — the model is cached, so sorting works with little or no signal — essential in Kampala's connectivity reality.

This turns every citizen's phone into a recycling assistant, directly enabling the source-separation that SDG 12.5 calls for.

8. Impact Model

EcoCycle creates impact through a reinforcing cycle rather than a single intervention:

  1. Visibility — citizen reports surface uncollected and illegally-dumped waste that the current system never sees.
  2. Action — those reports become trackable collector jobs with proof, raising effective collection coverage above today's ~59%.
  3. Diversion — AI sorting plus rewards pull recyclables and organics out of the landfill stream, cutting both volume and methane.
  4. Measurement — the city dashboard quantifies progress against SDG targets, enabling data-driven decisions.

Every recyclable diverted reduces pressure on overstretched sites like the one that collapsed at Kiteezi, and every tonne of organics kept out of landfill cuts greenhouse-gas emissions.

10. Security & Engineering

  • Password security: bcrypt hashing — plaintext passwords are never stored.
  • Session security: signed JWTs with a server-side secret; protected API routes verify the token.
  • Account verification: OTP (one-time passcode) and Google Sign-In.
  • Safe uploads: photo uploads handled by multer and served from an isolated path.
  • Resilience: a service worker provides offline operation; the API and static app share one secure origin.
  • Configurable integrations: SMS, email and payments switch from sandbox to live purely through environment variables — secrets stay out of the codebase.

11. Scalability & Future Work

  • Multi-city rollout — the role model and data schema generalise to any municipality beyond Kampala.
  • IoT smart bins — fill-level sensors can feed the same dashboard for predictive collection.
  • Waste-to-resource partnerships — integrate composting, recycling and waste-to-energy off-takers to monetise diverted material.
  • Government & KCCA integration — official adoption would turn citizen reports into a formal service channel.
  • Carbon credits — quantified landfill diversion could be packaged as verifiable climate impact.

12. Conclusion

Kampala's waste crisis is a tragedy of broken information as much as of insufficient trucks. EcoCycle rebuilds that information loop in software that already works today — connecting every citizen, collector and city official around shared, intelligent, incentivised waste management. By advancing SDGs 11, 12 and 13 with a real, deployable, offline-capable platform, EcoCycle offers a credible, scalable path from disasters like Kiteezi toward genuinely smart, clean cities.
